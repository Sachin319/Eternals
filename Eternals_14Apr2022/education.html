Diamonds have characteristics that determine their rarity. This can be broken down into the four C’s.

Weight, Not Size

A carat is a measurement of the total weight of a diamond, not size. One carat equals 0.20 grams. The higher the carat of a diamond, the heavier its weight, making it appear bigger in size.
Out of all the four C’s, carat has the biggest influence on a diamond’s price. Since high-carat diamonds come from rough crystals with hard-to-find sizes, this rarity makes them more expensive to buy.

For Total Transparency

When it comes to determining the value of a diamond, color is a way of showing us the quality of a diamond from an atomic standpoint. Cheaper diamonds tend to have a yellowish hue. Eternals Jewelry offers diamonds that appear colorless to the eye, emphasizing their sparkle. The less color a diamond has, the more valuable it is. A diamond that has a higher purity and more perfect structure will show less color, and rank higher on a grading system. This scale begins at “D” for the very best diamonds, and drops down to “Z” for the lowest-rated diamonds. Due to the need for special tools and training, certified grading professionals determine a diamond’s color grade.

Notice how a diamond shines so brightly? That’s the diamond’s cut calling to you.

Cut describes the way a finished diamond is crafted to give it maximum sparkle. Despite being commonly mistaken for shape, cut is a way of describing how well a diamond interacts with light to shine with brilliance.

A diamond featuring an ideal cut has a magnificent sparkle that dazzles the eyes; light entering the diamond’s crown from the top will bounce right back out due to its perfect symmetry and polish. A diamond with a poor cut won’t shine as brightly. Too deep of a cut, and light will escape to the sides; too shallow, and light will escape through its pavilion.

A poorly cut diamond won’t shine well even if it has a large carat weight and is colorless. On the other hand, a well-cut diamond will show sparkle, fire, and brilliance – no matter its size. And since it is the only one of the four C’s decided by human interaction, the cut of a diamond is very important towards its quality.

Although the market is filled with all types of diamond cuts, Eternals Jewelry only sells “Excellent” diamonds.

Crystal Clear: Why is Clarity Important to Diamonds?

 

To figure out the clarity of a diamond, a trained diamond specialist uses a magnified jeweler’s eyepiece called a Loupe to examine its interior and exterior for imperfections and flaws.

Flaws on the surface are called blemishes, while ones located on the inside of a diamond are called inclusions.

Eternals Jewelry prides itself on only offering diamonds with the very best clarity grades. We only offer flawless, VVS, and VS.

FL

FL:Flawless diamonds have no internal or external inclusions. Less than 1% of diamonds have flawless clarity, and are extremely rare.

VVS diamonds

VVS diamonds: have miniscule and difficult to see inclusions under 10x magnification, and are very rare. Even for a trained eye, the inclusions are difficult to see.

VS diamonds

VS diamonds have minor inclusions that are difficult to see under 10x magnification. Minor inclusions are undetectable to the unaided eye. VS diamonds are popular for their great value.

SI diamonds

SI diamonds: Inclusions are noticeable at 10x magnification, and can even be detectable to the naked eye. SI diamonds don’t shine as brightly as higher offerings. Eternals does not carry SI diamonds as they don’t meet our high standards.

I clarity diamonds

I clarity diamonds: have obvious inclusions that impact beauty and sparkle. These inclusions are visible to the naked eye. Eternals does not carry I diamonds as they don’t meet our high standards.

All diamonds sold by Eternals Jewelry are certified and graded by the world’s top diamond accreditors. Our diamonds are GIA or IGI approved, and a report is given with each diamond purchase.

The Gemological Institute of America (GIA) is regarded as one of the leading diamond accreditors in the world. The GIA is a trusted international industry standard on diamond quality.

The International Gemological Institute (IGI) is a global leader for jewelry assessment. IGI is the largest organization of its kind, operating 20 laboratory locations around the world grading finished jewelry, natural diamonds, lab-grown diamonds and gemstones.
